CFPB reports a 37% increase in credit card complaints in just 5 months



by Maria Socorro Garcia



Complaints about credit cards rose from 65,000 to 89,000, a 37% increase in October from May 2017, the Consumer Finance Protection Board reports complaints on its website.

Complaints received by Credit Card Issuers per 10,000 Credit Card Holders


Synchrony Financial receives 2.4 complaints for every 10,000 holders of their plastic card. Discover and American Express receives the least number of complaints from their customers at 1.1 for every 10,000 holders of their own.





Credit Card Companies Most Likely to Respond with Refunds


Although Citibank garnered the most complaints, it had the highest percentage when responding back with monetary refunds.





Credit Card Complaints by Issue


The 3 topmost issues complained about were billing disputes, identity theft and a category called ‘other’. Billing disputes accounted for 17% of the total complaints while identity theft/fraud/embezzlement complaints were at 10%. The only thing unique was American Express, which showed rewards as its second most complained about issue. 14% of Amex complaints were about rewards.
